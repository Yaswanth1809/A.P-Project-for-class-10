ARITHMETIC PROGRESSIONS

Definition of arithmetic progression

An arithmetic progression is a list of numbers in which each term is obtained by adding a fixed number to the preceding term except the first term

This fixed number is called the common difference of the AP. Remember that it can be positive,negative or zero.

Let us denote the first term of an AP by a1

second term as a2

and so on nth term as an

common difference as d

Example: The heights (in cm) of some students of a school standing in a queue in the morning assembly are 147 , 148, 149, . . ., 157.This is an example of positive common difference

Example: The balance money after paying 5 % of the total loan of 1000 every month is 950, 900, 850, 800, . . ., 50...This is an example of Negative common difference

Various terms in A.P and Formula sheet

Nth term or General Term of A.P:
first Term: a
Second Term: a + d
nth term: a+(n-1)*d

Sum of first n terms of A.P(Denoted by Sn): (n/2)*[2a + (n - 1)*d]

Last term is denoted by l

Sum of nterms of A.P if last term is Known: (n/2)*[a + l]

Last term when sum of terms is known: Sn - Sn-1

Common Difference : an - an-1

if a,b,c are in a.p then b = (a+c)/2.Here b is called the arithmetic mean of a and c

sum of first n positive integers is given by [n*(n+1)]/2

Some Solved Examples for nth Term:

Example1: Find the 10th term of the AP : 2, 7, 12, . . .
Solution: a = 2, d = 7 – 2 = 5 and n = 10
an= a + (n – 1) d
a10= 2 + (10 – 1) × 5 = 2 + 45 = 47

Example2: Which term of the AP : 21, 18, 15, . . . is – 81?
Solution: Here, a = 21, d = 18 – 21 = – 3 and an= – 81, and we have to find n.
an = a + ( n – 1) d,
–81 = 21 + (n – 1)(–3)
–81 = 24 – 3n
–105 =–3n
so n = 35
Therefore, the 35th term of the given AP is –81.

Example3: Determine the AP whose 3rd term is 5 and the 7th term is 9.
Solution: a3= a + (3 – 1) d = a + 2d = 5
a7 = a + (7 – 1) d = a + 6d = 9
Solving the pair of linear equations (1) and (2), we get a = 3 and d = 1
Hence, the required AP is 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, . . .

Some solved Examples for sum of n terms:

Example1: Find the sum of the first 22 terms of the AP : 8, 3, –2, . . .
Solution: Here, a = 8, d = 3 – 8 = –5, n = 22
S = (n/2)*[2a + (n - 1)*d]
S = (22/2)*[16 + 21(-5)]
S = 11[16 - 105]
Answer is -979

Example2: If the sum of the first 14 terms of an AP is 1050 and its first term is 10, find the 20th term.
Solution: Here, S14 = 1050, n = 14, a =10
As, Sn = (n/2)[2a + (n – 1)d]
So,
1050 = (14/2)[20 + 13d] = 140 + 91d
i.e., 910 = 91d
d = 10
Therefore, a20 = 10 + (20 – 1) × 10 = 200
Hence, 20th term = 200.

Example3: How many terms of the AP: 24, 21, 18, … must be taken so that their sum is 78?
Solution: Here, a = 24, d = 21 – 24 = –3, Sn = 78
We need to find n.
Sn = (n/2)[2a + (n – 1)d]
78 = (n/2)[48 + (n – 1)(–3)]
78 = (n/2)[48 – 3n + 3]
78 = (n/2)(51 – 3n)
156 = n(51 – 3n)
3n2 – 51n + 156 = 0
Divide the whole equation by 3:
n2 – 17n + 52 = 0
Solving the quadratic:
(n – 13)(n – 4) = 0
n = 4 or 13
Sum becomes 78 for the smaller value
Hence, number of terms = 4.

Some Solved Examples for real life applications:

Example 1:A sum of ₹1000 is invested at 8% simple interest per year.Calculate the interest at the end of each year.Do these interests form an AP?If so, find the interest at the end of 30 years making use of this fact.
Solution: We know that the formula to calculate simple interest is:
Simple Interest = (P × R × T)/100
So, the interest at the end of the 1st year = (1000 × 8 × 1) / 100 = ₹80
The interest at the end of the 2nd year = (1000 × 8 × 2) / 100 = ₹160
The interest at the end of the 3rd year = (1000 × 8 × 3) / 100 = ₹240
Similarly, we can obtain the interest at the end of the 4th year, 5th year, and so on.
So, the interest (in ₹) at the end of the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, … years, respectively are:
80, 160, 240, …
It is an AP as the difference between consecutive terms is 80, i.e., d = 80.Also, a = 80.
So, to find the interest at the end of 30 years, we calculate a30.
a30 = a + (30 – 1)d = 80 + 29 × 80 = 2400
Hence, the interest at the end of 30 years is ₹2400.

Example 2: In a flower bed, there are 23 rose plants in the first row, 21 in the second, 19 in the third, and so on. There are 5 rose plants in the last row. How many rows are there in the flower bed?
Solution:The number of rose plants in the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, . . ., rows are : 23, 21, 19, . . ., 5
It forms an AP (Why?). Let the number of rows in the flower bed be n.
Then, a=23, d = 21–23 = –2,an=5
an=a+(n-1)*d
5 = 23 + (n – 1)(– 2)
– 18 = (n – 1)(– 2)
n = 10
So, there are 10 rows in the flower bed.

Example 3: A manufacturer of TV sets produced 600 sets in the third year and 700sets in the seventh year. Assuming that the production increases uniformly by a fixednumber every year, find : 1.The production in the 1st year,2.The production in the 10th year,3.The total production in first 7 years
Solution:1. Since the production increases uniformly by a fixed number every year,the number of TV sets manufactured in 1st, 2nd, 3rd, . . ., years will form an AP.Let us denote the number of TV sets manufactured in the nth year by an.
a3=600 and a7=700 or a + 2d = 600 and a+6d = 700
Solving these equations, we get d = 25 and a = 550.
Therefore, production of TV sets in the first year is 550.
2. a10 = a+9d = 550+9*25 = 775
So, production of TV sets in the 10th year is 775.
3. s7= 7/2[2*550 + (7-1)*25] = 7/2[1100+150] = 4375
Thus, the total production of TV sets in first 7 years is 4375.

Question 3:Which term of the A.P. : 65, 61, 57, 53, _____ is the first negative term?

Case Based Questions:

Question 1:A school is organizing a charity run to raise funds for a local hospital. The run is planned as a series of rounds around a track, with each round being 300 metres. To make the event more challenging and engaging, the organizers decide to increase the distance of each subsequent round by 50 metres. For example,the second round will be 350 metres, the third round will be 400 metres and so on. The total number of rounds planned is 10.

Based on the given information answer the following questions:
(i) Write the fourth, fifth and sixth term of the Arithmetic Progression so formed.
(ii) Determine the distance of the 8th round.
(iii) (a) Find the total distance run after completing all 10 rounds.(or)
(iii) (b) If a runner completes only the first 6 rounds, what is the total distance run by the runner?

(i) The Arithmetic progression so formed will be 300, 350, 400, 450, ..... up to 10 terms.
a4 = 450; a5 = 500; a6 = 550
(ii) Here, a = 300, d = 50, n = 8
an = a + (n - 1)d
a8 = 300 + (8 - 1)50 = 300 + 7(50) = 300 + 350 = 650
(iii) (a) We have, a = 300, d = 50, n = 10, then total distance run after completing all 10 rounds is given by
s10 = 10/2[600 + 9*50] = 5[600+450] = 5250 meters.
(iii) (b) We have, a = 300, d = 50, n = 6
If a runner completes only first 6 rounds, then total distance run is given by n
s6 = 6/2[600+5*50] = 3[600+250] = 2550 meters.

Question 2::In order to organise Annual Sports Day, a school prepared an eight lane running track with an integrated football field inside the track area as shown below:

The length of innermost lane of the track is 400 m and each subsequent lane is 7.6 m longer than the preceding lane.
Based on given information, answer the following questions, using concept of Arithmetic Progression.
(i) What is the length of the 6th lane?
(ii) How long is the 8th lane than that of 4th lane?

(iii) (b) A student took one round each in lane 4 to lane 8. Find the total distance covered by the student.

(i) We have, a= 400 m, d = 7.6 m
Length of 6th lane(a6) = a + (6 - 1)d =a + 5d
= 400 + 5(7.6) = 438 m
(ii) Length of 8th lane, a8 = a + 7d
= 400 + 7(7.6) = 453.2 m
Length of 4th lane, a4 = a+ 3d = 400 + 3(7.6) = 422.8 m
Difference = (453.2 - 422. 8) m = 30.4 m
(iii) (a) Total distance covered by a student in first six lanes, is given by, 6
s6 = 6/2[2*400+5*7.6]= 3(800+38)= 2514m(or)
(iii) (b) Total distance covered by a student from lane 4 to lane 8, is, S8 - S3
s8-s3 = 8/2[2a+7d] - 3/2[2a+2d]
= 4(2a + 7d) - 3(a+ d) = 8a + 28d - 3a - 3d
= 5a + 25d = 5(400) + 25(7.6) [∵ a= 400, d = 7.6]
= 2000 + 190 = 2190 m
